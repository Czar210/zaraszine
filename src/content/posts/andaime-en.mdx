This post **is not content** — it's a scaffold generated in M1 to demonstrate the format. Delete it (and its twin `andaime-pt.mdx`) once you write for real. A new post is just an `.mdx` file in this folder.

## What this file proves

That an `.mdx` with `titulo`, `resumo`, `data`, `lang`, `tags` and (optional) `traducao` becomes a navigable post, with a procedural cover generated from the title, and no other step.

![Sample figure, on the right](/media/figura-2.svg)

The text breathes in a reading column. Drop a big image and it **bleeds outward and alternates sides** — that's the body's Z-shape, and it happens on its own from the CSS. You mark nothing: just write plain markdown.

## Features already working

- Deterministic procedural cover (reload: it's identical).
- Language stamp on the meta line. Since this post has a `traducao`, it and the Portuguese twin carry the double **PT✳EN** stamp.
- The switch at the top, on a post with a twin, jumps straight to the other language.
